Chocolate Balls

Chokladbollar are absolute classics of the Swedish bakery. Chocolate balls are standard in most Swedish cafés and it is hard to imagine the confectioners’ refrigerated display cases without them. These chocolate balls from Sweden are not only very delicious and savoury, but they are also very easy to prepare. Almost every Swede has eaten or prepared chokladbollar at some time or another. For many Swedish parents it is customary to introduce children to baking and kneading the dough together, and rolling it into balls. A quick sense of achievement, and above all tasty.

Ingredients

  • 150 g room temp butter
  • 2 dl sugar
  • 1.5 tsp vanilla sugar
  • 4-5 tsp unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 3.5 dl oat flakes
  • 1 dl grated coconut
  • 2-3 tbsp stong, cold coffee

Garnish

  • Coconut flakes
  • Alternative: Granulated sugar

Steps

  1. Mix the soft butter, sugar and vanilla sugar.
  2. Add cocoa, oat flakes and cold coffee and mix well.
  3. Roll the finished tough dough into small balls. If the dough is too soft, you can let it cool down a little in the fridge before rolling it.
  4. Then roll the balls on a plate of granulated sugar or coconut flakes to evenly distribute the garnish on each ball.
  5. Keep them in the refrigerator for about 1 hour before eating them.

Shelf life: in the refrigerator at least 3 weeks, in the freezer at least 3 months.
